Mozart Works

The composer

“The idea grows more and more, I develop it and explain it; The work is almost ready in my mind, even if it is long, so that later, I embrace it in my spirit with a single glance, like a beautiful painting or a beautiful man, and I listen to it in my imagination, not in succession. in which it should appear, if not in one fell swoop, in its entirety. What a feast! All the work of imagination and elaboration is carried out in me as in a deep and beautiful dream, whose best aspect is that kind of global vision ”(Mozart). The "divine Mozart" left a mystery around him: his precocity, his speed of writing, the perfection of his harmony that never collides, are so many questions. He seemed driven by the need to produce, as if he sensed the brevity of his existence. Although he wrote to order and out of material necessity, Mozart successfully tackled all genres of works. The number of his works is prodigious (more than 600), although difficult to determine exactly, due to the lost scores and doubts concerning the authenticity of certain pages of his catalog.
